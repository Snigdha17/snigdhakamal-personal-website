The case against Instagram Kids

September 29, 2021

Two days ago, Facebook announced that they were pausing the development of their Instagram Kids app, which was supposed to be a version of Instagram built for children under the age of 13. The pause was probably due to the backlash from policymakers, regulators and child safety groups who were concerned about the rampant bullying and body shaming that children (especially girls) face on social media.
However, FB's stance on this is that building a social media app for kids is still the "right thing to do", and they are only pausing development to figure out what the app should be like. This stance is something I cannot agree with. Apps such as Instagram work on the "Hook" model, which relies on a cycle of "Trigger-action-reward-investment" to literally hook users by playing on humans' need for dopamine. The idea that it's okay to expose kids to this cycle from as young an age as 10-11 years, when their self control is not even fully formed, is extremely harmful. Early addiction to dopamine triggers will only make children more and more dependent on things that provide instant gratification, and make it even harder to control compulsive disorders such as addiction and obesity.
